# Privacy Policy for LittleMind AI

**Last Updated: April 13, 2026**

At **LittleMind AI** (referred to as "we," "us," or "our"), we are committed to protecting the privacy of our users, especially children. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and safeguard personal information when you use our mobile application ("the App").

---

## 1. Information We Collect

### 1.1 Personal Information
To provide a personalized experience for your child, the App allows you to provide:
- **Child's Name**: Used to address the child personally during AI interactions.
- **Child's Age**: Used to adjust the complexity and safety filters of the AI responses.
- **Interests**: Used to tailor educational content and stories to the child’s preferences.

### 1.2 Audio and Voice Data
The App uses a "Speech-to-Text" feature which requires access to the device's microphone. 
- **Audio Processing**: When the microphone is active, audio is recorded and processed locally or sent to an AI provider for transcription into text.
- **Usage**: We do NOT store raw audio files on our servers. The text transcription is used solely to interact with the AI assistant.

### 1.3 Usage Data
We may collect non-identifiable technical data such as device type, OS version, and app usage patterns (e.g., number of questions asked) to improve the App’s performance.

---

## 2. How We Use Information

We use the collected information for the following purposes:
- **Personalization**: To create a tailored educational and fun experience for your child.
- **Communication**: To allow the AI to respond to your child's voice and text inputs.
- **Safety**: To ensure AI responses are age-appropriate based on the child's profile.
- **App Improvement**: To debug issues and optimize the user interface.

---

## 3. Third-Party Services & AI Providers

To generate responses, the App sends text-based prompts to third-party AI providers selected by the user, such as:
- **Google Gemini**
- **OpenRouter**
- **Hugging Face**

**Important Disclosure**: While prompts are sent to these providers, they are sent without the child's full profile (only age-relevant context is included to ensure safety). We recommend reviewing the privacy policies of these providers if you configure your own API keys in the App.

---

## 4. Kids and Parent Care Section (COPPA & Safety)

### 4.1 COPPA Compliance
LittleMind AI is designed with the **Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA)** in mind. We prioritize child safety above all else.

### 4.2 Parental Controls
- **API Management**: Parents have exclusive control over the AI provider settings and API keys.
- **Profile Management**: Parents can update or delete the child’s profile (Name, Age, Interests) at any time through the Settings menu.
- **Offline Mode**: The App supports local AI processing (via Ollama) for parents who prefer that no data leaves the device.

### 4.3 AI Safety & Filtering
We implement strict system instructions for the AI to ensure:
- **No Inappropriate Content**: The AI is instructed to remain helpful, polite, and age-appropriate.
- **Privacy Education**: The AI is programmed to NEVER ask a child for sensitive personal details like their physical address, school name, or phone number.

### 4.4 Data Minimization
We only collect the absolute minimum data required to make the app functional and personalized. We do NOT sell any data to third parties.

---

## 5. Data Retention & Deletion

- **Local Storage**: Most data (Name, Age, Interests, Chat History) is stored locally on your device via `SharedPreferences`.
- **Deletion**: You can clear all app data by going to your device's app settings or by using the "Delete History" feature within the App.
- **Account-Free**: We do not require a central account to use the App, meaning we do not maintain a permanent database of your personal information on our own servers.

---

## 6. Security

We implement industry-standard security measures (such as HTTPS encryption for API calls) to protect your data. However, no method of transmission over the internet is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.
